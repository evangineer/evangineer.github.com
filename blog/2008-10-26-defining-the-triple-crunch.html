Defining the Triple Crunch

Plenty of people must be looking at various crises and be wondering what on earth is going on. In my view, we are facing an almost unprecedented Triple Crunch.

We are all probably sick of hearing about the Credit Crunch_. What’s getting less attention but is still making an impact on our wallets is a food and fuel crunch. This is due to high oil prices which has a domino effect on a system of industrialized food production which is dependent on petrochemicals for fertilizers and distribution to consumers. However you define it, the majority of people (ie low to middle income earners) have genuine reasons to be gravely concerned on a number of fronts. In doing so, most people are overlooking the opportunity to reconsider what is thought of as “business as usual” in economic and political affairs. The prevailing models of neoliberal capitalism and representative democracy have both failed, just as communism failed with the collapse of the Berlin Wall. We need more just than a Green New Deal but it would certainly be a good start. Rather than repeat past mistakes, how can we move to the next level of human civilisation? One that ensures that everyone has enough to eat, a decent roof over their heads, enough energy for their needs, a proper say in the decisions that affect their lives and the opportunity to reach their full potential to contribute to society.

Given that it is Crunch Time for humanity and the planet, perhaps we should consider Plan B or even Plan C?

PS Looks like a water crunch may be in the works, according to experts attending this year’s Stockholm International Water Conference.
