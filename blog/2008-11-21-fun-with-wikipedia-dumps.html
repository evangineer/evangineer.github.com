Fun with Wikipedia dumps

tav was downloading a complete dump of the contents of Wikipedia and I was asking him what he would do with it. He wanted to be able to use it offline and found an article on building a fast Wikipedia offline reader.

I pointed out the much smaller dump of the Appropedia sustainability wiki, which he used to test the reader after making some small tweaks. The test was successful, he was able to search for articles and read them. One thing that didn’t work was images, it should be possible to tweak the scripts to either pull in the images using ajax style trickery or instead provide working links to the images on the Appropedia site.

I have a long standing interest in being able to repurpose the content of sites like Wikipedia and Appropedia. A DIY Guide for rebuilding Human Civilisation might come in useful, you never know.

Incidentally, the Transition Handbook has been made availble on Appropedia by the founder of the Transition Towns movement, Rob Hopkins.
