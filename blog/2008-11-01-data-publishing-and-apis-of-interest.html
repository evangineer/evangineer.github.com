Data Publishing and APIs of Interest

Via Hacker News, I discovered the World Bank data API. On pointing it out to tav, he noted that there was also a Reuters API. A bit of searching turned up the World Health Organisation’s World Health Statistics 2007 found via Data/Blog. These kinds of data sets and apis fit in with one of my interests which how to represent otherwise boring and uninteresting data in visually engaging and exciting ways. That sort of thing has been called data publishing.

I’ve experimented with SIMILE Exhibit (down at the time of writing) in the past using datasets from Plan B 3.0 and am keen to also try out the Trendalyzer technology developed by Gapminder and currently owned by Google who have dubbed it Motion Chart and made it part of their Google Visualization API.

I’m interested in using things like Exhibit and Motion Chart to publish data in my blog entries. Guess this would be data blogging, but apparently the term datablogging has been claimed by Joe Reger for a somewhat different purpose. His usage which approximates to personal datamining similar to Daytum inspired and designed by Nicholas Felton in the spirit of his Annual Reports. Whereas I’m more interested in working with international and global datasets.
