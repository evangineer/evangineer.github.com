Personal Data Tracking

An emergent form of website is one that like Daytum and Mycrocosm allows users to track and visualize personal data that can be as trivial as how many cups of coffee they have during the day and or as critical as how many times they have taken their heart medicine.

Out of the various classes of data that can be tracked, it seems to me the ones most worthwhile are to do with wellbeing, finances and ecological footprint. In order to reduce what might otherwise be an onerous data entry burden, a dedicated client that runs on the user’s mobile phone and uploads data to the website via its api. Such a client can exploit on-device capabilities such as GPS and accelerometers in order to obtain data from which inferences can be drawn about the behaviour of the user.

An interesting twist could be to not only track the good habits that a user is trying to cultivate but also to prompt the user to perform the actions that the good habits consist of.

Update: A list of 23 Personal Tools to Learn More About Yourself and from MetaFilter, some critiques of the forms of graphing being used to visualize data. Twitter can be abused for data collection too like tracking your weight.
