The Economies of Agility and Disrupting the Nature of the Firm

In the industrial age, mass production by firms exploited the economies of scale. We are however transitioning to a postindustrial age, the network age where emerging Peer Production will be driven by the economies of agility. Economies of scale are about driving down costs of manufactured goods by producing them on a large scale. Economies of agility in contrast are about quickly being able to switch between producing different goods and services in response to demand. We see an early variant of this in Dell’s build to order model which disrupted the Personal Computer industry.
