Blueprint for a better world: Beware of common sense

UK’s New Scientist magazine is running a series of articles called Blueprint for a better world looking at various ideas for making the world a better place. Given that this is a specialist topic of mind, I thought it might be interesting to evaluate them.

The first article Beware of common sense makes the case for evidence-based government. Given that it has been a number of centuries since The Enlightenment, if it’s not a standard practice by now, there is clearly something wrong with our political cultures. That being the case, perhaps we need to look at the education of public servants including politicians and policy-makers, ensuring that they have enough of an indoctrination into the Scientific Method that evidence-based government is the rule rather than the exception.

An alternative and perhaps more radical move is specifically create a place for scientists and engineers in our democratic structures. So alongside the House of Commons and House of Lords, there would be a House of Technocrats! Alongside Congress and the Senate, the Academy. That’s not to say there aren’t deeper problems with our democracies that won’t be solved by this. There are, but at least this would embed an evidence-based approach more deeply into our democratic structures.
